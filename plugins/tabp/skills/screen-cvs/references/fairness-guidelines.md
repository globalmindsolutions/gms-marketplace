# Fairness & Bias Guidelines

CV screening affects people's livelihoods. These rules are mandatory on every run. They keep the assessment job-relevant, consistent, and defensible, and they keep the human recruiter — not the model — accountable for the decision.

## Evaluate only job-relevant qualifications

Score a candidate **solely** on skills, experience, competencies, education, and other criteria that the JD actually requires. If a detail does not bear on the ability to do the job described, it must not influence the score.

## Never use protected characteristics or their proxies

Do **not** infer, mention, score on, or let your reasoning be influenced by:

- Age or date of birth
- Gender or gender identity
- Race, ethnicity, or skin color
- National origin or citizenship — **except** explicit work-authorization requirements the JD actually states
- Religion or beliefs
- Disability or health/medical information
- Marital status, pregnancy, or family/caregiving status
- Sexual orientation
- Political affiliation or union membership
- Physical appearance / photographs

**Common proxies to ignore as well:**

- **Graduation or early-career dates** used to estimate age — judge experience by relevance and duration, not by when it started.
- **Names, nationality, or location** used to infer ethnicity, gender, or origin.
- **Photos** on the CV.
- **Club/affiliation memberships** that signal a protected trait without job relevance.

## Handle gaps and non-linear histories neutrally

- Note an employment gap factually if relevant to recency of skills; **do not penalize** it or speculate about the reason.
- Career changes, returnships, and non-traditional paths are not negatives in themselves. Judge the resulting skills.

## Be consistent

Apply the **same** requirement list, weights, and evidence standard to every candidate in a batch. Inconsistent criteria are a fairness failure and make any ranking invalid.

## Flag problems in the JD itself

If the JD contains a requirement that is not job-relevant or is potentially discriminatory (e.g., an age range, "native speaker", gender terms, unnecessary physical requirements), **do not silently score against it**. Surface it to the user and suggest a job-relevant alternative where possible.

## Be transparent and humble

- Cite the evidence behind every Met/Partial/Missing judgment.
- When evidence is thin or ambiguous, **say so** and lower confidence rather than guessing.
- Present results as **decision-support**: state explicitly that the final hiring decision rests with the human recruiter.

## Respect privacy

Treat CV contents as confidential. Surface only the candidate details the user needs for the screening (e.g., name and fit); do not expand or share personal data beyond the task.
